Research areas and projects

Our main areas involves Environmental monitoring, Detection of hazardous compounds, Technology for defense, Technology for aerospace and the Development of equipment. In most areas you can find several microsystems, sensors and biosensors, as well as nanotech techniques. Some of these projects are undergoing tech transfer and entrepreneurship.

Environmental monitoring is an area that comprises gas detection (e.g. O2, COx, SOx) and waste monitoring (e.g. biomass, biogas production). Our team developes sensors and biosensors for these purposes using MOx (Metal-Oxide), IR (Infrared), polymeric nanolayers, surface functionalization and microsystem fabrication technologies. In addition, we develop specific electronics to incorporate there sensors into field-equipment.

Gas Sensors
Development of gas microsensor using MOx (Metal-Oxide), surface functionalization and polymeric nanolayers is one of our main components in these area. However, our technology is not limitated by those techniques. IR (Infrared) technology is a promising technology in the detection and quantification of gases. Until now, we have developed several gas sensors using multiple technologies, our brochure includes oxygen, COx, H2S and SOx gases.

Micro-respirometer
We develope new equipment and technology for in-situ measurements of biomass in waste treatment plants and farms. Our system is capable of establishing the biomass of a real sample in a matter of 90 minutes without the need of labs or experts. The complete equipment measures the biomass of different samples and gives the detailes information in electronic format so it can be downloaded using a USB port. The measuring device is a low cost (<$5 USD) disposable element while the reading equipment is fully portable.

Bioreactors
Using the microfabrication technology, we can produce different and configurable bioreactors on glass or transparent polymers to grow bacteria, yeast, fungi or algea. Our technology has been tested for several strains and can be complemented with passive microelectronics to detect variations of the cultures.

Detection of hazardous compounds includes the development of sensors and biosensors to quantify or identify compounds in solution. These sensors are based on enzymes, antibodies or living microorganisms. In addition, the development of technologies, equipment, tools and artifacts for blood stream analysis and medical training.

Virus detection biomicrosystems
Development of a biomicrosystem that allows more than 90 simultaneous analysis for the detection of the HPV (Human papilloma virus) in-situ. The biomicrosystem is portable, inexpensive and simple to use and read.

Biosensors for the detection of hazardous compounds in water
Development of different biosensors, based on enzymes, living and confined microorganisms and MOx (Metal Oxide) technology for the detection and quantification of hazardous compounds, such as phenols and heavy metals, in liquid samples.

Optical recognition system for microfluidic analysis and blood stream studies
Development of an optical recognition system with microfluidics manipulation for real-time analysis of blood samples, drug delivery inside artificial arteries and 3D reconstruction of microfluidics, fluid analysis and particle tracking. It also includes the development of artificial transparent arteries for medical studies.

Technology for defense includes the development of sensors, fabric and suit manipulation and electronic systems integrated as interdisciplinary solutions. All the developed technology are compatible and can be integrated into different devices and platforms.

Explosive Sensors
Development of explosive material by the detection of gases. Our technology is based on MOx (Metal Oxide) and surface functionalization technology and elastomers or glass-based substrates. The sensors are complemented by the designed electronics for close-range wireless technology or portable electronics.

Hydrophobic and water resistant nanotechnology
Based on nanotechnology surface modification we have developed a technique for making fabrics and leather superhydrophobis. The technology has a nanometer size and it is clear yellow. Part of this technology has been transfered to one of our start up companies: nanoTactics.

Electronics communication systems
Development of short and mid-range communication systems integrated into suits and accesories. These systems can be integrated to sensor technology.

Technology for aerospace includes the development of microsystems and special electronics for aerospace crafts. Also, together with different Schools and Departments of los Andes University, we participate in the Andes Aerospace Project (PUA). We are in charge of the electronic components that comprises the real-time communication system, sensors, processors, electric power and ignition control.

The development of Equipment and Technology includes the design, manufacture and fabrication of different equipment and infrastructure for the Clean Room and other laboratories. This area includes a section of "Do It Yourself" (DIY) that provides open access documents.
